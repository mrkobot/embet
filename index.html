emBET scans live lines across DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, and others daily.
The edge shown is the gap between a book's price and Pinnacle's, where Pinnacle is available — de-vigged with Shin's method (corrects for favorite-longshot bias) and filtered to a 1.5%+ gap. It's a measure of price disagreement against a sharp reference, not a prediction that the bet wins more often than it's priced to.
This finds disagreement between books, not independent predictions — it doesn't know about injuries, weather, or lineups. Average CLV over a large sample is the real test of whether that disagreement is worth anything; any single result is noise.
